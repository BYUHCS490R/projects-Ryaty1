Linus Torvalds

Linus Torvalds is the creator of Linux, a widely used open-source operating system. He is known for his strong beliefs in open-source software and has made significant contributions to the tech community. His work has inspired countless developers and has had a lasting impact on the world of technology. As an IT student I admire his dedication to innovation and collaboration in the tech industry.

Key Achievements

Created Linux
Linus Torvalds created the Linux kernel in 1991, which has since become the foundation for many operating systems worldwide.
Founder of Git
Linus Torvalds initiated the development of Git in 2005 as a distributed version control system to manage the Linux kernel's source code.
Advocate for Open Source
Linus Torvalds has been a vocal advocate for open-source software, promoting collaboration and transparency in software development.
