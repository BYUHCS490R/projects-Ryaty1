Welcome to Community Nutrition

Community Nutrition is a friendly place to learn simple facts about the foods you can grow in your backyard or find at a local market.

The goal of this site is to collect clear information about everyday vegetables and basic nutrition so families can feel more confident when they cook, garden, and plan meals.

On each page, you will find short explanations, helpful tips, and ideas you can try at home, along with references to trusted nutrition resources.

Protein-rich foods help build and repair body tissues, while the amount of fat and calories in each meat can make a big difference in daily energy and long-term health. This simple table compares a few common meats so you can see how they differ when you are planning a balanced meal.

Approximate nutrition per 100 grams of cooked meat
Meat Protein (g) Total Fat (g) Energy (kcal)
Chicken breast, skinless 31 3.6 165
Chicken thigh, roasted 25 9 209
Beef steak, grilled (lean) 26 15 250
Pork chops, broiled (lean) 26 13 242
Salmon, baked 25 14 232
Egg, fried (1 large) 13 10 196

Nutrition values are approximate and adapted from USDA FoodData Central and summary tables from MyFoodData, which compile data from the USDA database for common meats.

Remember that numbers only tell part of the story. Lean meats and fish can provide protein with less saturated fat, and pairing them with plenty of vegetables, beans, and whole grains can make a complete and satisfying meal.
